### CIA-Style Intelligence Analysis
```
Analyze {situation/threat/opportunity} using structured analytical techniques:

ANALYTICAL FRAMEWORK:
1. **Key Assumptions Check**: What must be true for this to matter?
2. **Alternative Hypotheses**: Generate 4-5 competing explanations
3. **Inconsistent Evidence**: What contradicts the leading hypothesis?
4. **Deception Check**: How might we be deliberately misled?

INFORMATION EVALUATION:
- Source credibility: Track record, access, motivation
- Information reliability: Corroboration, logical consistency  
- Recency and relevance: Time-decay factors
- Collection gaps: What don't we know that matters?

BIAS MITIGATION:
- Confirmation bias: Actively seek disconfirming evidence
- Availability heuristic: Weight base rates over vivid examples
- Anchoring: Consider multiple starting points
- Groupthink: Red team the consensus view

CONFIDENCE ASSESSMENT:
- High confidence: Multiple independent sources, consistent with patterns
- Moderate confidence: Some gaps but logical consistency
- Low confidence: Limited sources, significant uncertainties

STRATEGIC WARNING INDICATORS:
- What early signals would indicate change?
- Threshold events that would shift probability
- Collection requirements for better assessment

Output: Analytical judgment with confidence intervals and key uncertainties.
Think like a senior CIA analyst briefing the President.
```

### Red Team Your Own Thinking
```
Red team your analysis of {topic/decision/strategy}:

COGNITIVE VULNERABILITY AUDIT:
1. **Confirmation Bias**: What evidence contradicts your view?
2. **Availability Heuristic**: Are you overweighting recent/vivid examples?
3. **Anchoring**: How might your starting point bias conclusions?
4. **Overconfidence**: What's the range of possible outcomes?

ALTERNATIVE HYPOTHESIS GENERATION:
- Devil's advocate: Argue the opposite position
- Outside view: How do similar situations typically play out?
- Contrarian perspective: What would a smart skeptic say?
- Time horizon shift: How might this look in 1/5/10 years?

STRESS TESTING:
- Assumption relaxation: What if key assumptions are wrong?
- Resource constraints: What if budget/time were cut 50%?
- Competitive response: How would smart opponents react?
- Black swan events: What low-probability, high-impact events matter?

DECISION AUDIT:
- Process check: Did you follow best practices?
- Information quality: How reliable are your sources?
- Stakeholder alignment: Who hasn't been heard from?
- Reversibility: How hard is it to change course?

CALIBRATION TEST:
- Confidence intervals: Express uncertainty ranges
- Reference class forecasting: Base rates for similar decisions
- Pre-mortem analysis: How could this fail spectacularly?
- Success definition: What does "good outcome" mean?

Output: Refined analysis with explicit uncertainty and contingencies.
Think like a Supreme Court Justice examining your own reasoning.
```
